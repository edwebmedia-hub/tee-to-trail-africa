Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about planning a golf tour with Tee to Trail Africa.

  • We offer flexible invoicing, split deposits, or individual payments on request. We'll always agree on a clear payment schedule before any bookings are confirmed so there are no surprises.
  • We stagger tee times, set flights by pace and ability, and select tees and formats so that every player — from scratch handicapper to high-handicapper — genuinely enjoys the day. No one gets left behind.
  • We recommend confirming your trip 8–12 weeks ahead for the best availability at top courses and preferred accommodation. Short-notice bookings are possible but may have limited options, especially during peak season.
  • Absolutely — every tour we design is tailored to your group. We'll work with you on destinations, course selection, accommodation style, additional activities, transport, and budget to create an itinerary that fits perfectly.
  • The shoulder seasons — spring (September–October) and autumn (March–April) — offer the most pleasant conditions with mild weather and fewer crowds. That said, we run tours year-round and each season has its own charm.
  • A social, golf-first group trip with relaxed evenings, fun optional activities, and tailored logistics — designed to feel effortless for everyone in the group. Think great courses by day and good company by night.
  • We find that groups of 8–12 players hit the sweet spot for pricing, logistics, and atmosphere. That said, we cater to smaller groups from 4 players and larger corporate events too — just get in touch and we'll advise.
  • Yes — we design responsible stag itineraries with great golf, private dining, and carefully curated activities. We keep it memorable for the right reasons.
  • Absolutely. We add sightseeing, wine tours, spa treatments, and other activities so that non-golfers have a full and enjoyable programme while the group is on the course.
  • We recommend layers, sun protection, a windproof jacket, and comfortable walking shoes. Cape Town's weather can change quickly, especially on the Atlantic-facing courses where the wind can be a real factor in your game.
  • Yes — rental clubs and buggies can be pre-arranged at most courses. We always recommend bringing your own clubs if you can, but rental equipment is a solid option and we'll make sure it's sorted before you arrive.
  • Cape Town is one of the world's great cities — non-golfers will be spoilt for choice with Table Mountain, the V&A Waterfront, Boulders Beach penguins, wine tasting, whale watching, and world-class restaurants. We'll build a full programme for them.
  • Yes — Winelands itineraries naturally pair rounds with private wine tastings and estate lunches. Courses like Pearl Valley, De Zalze, and Devonvale are all set among working wine estates, making it effortless to combine the two.
  • Yes — we can arrange off-peak or private tastings and exclusive restaurant bookings at some of the Winelands' finest estates. These experiences are a highlight of any Winelands golf tour.
  • We typically recommend manor houses, boutique hotels, or private villas that are integrated into the wine estate experience. Staying on an estate adds a level of immersion that a standard hotel simply can't match.
  • It's one of South Africa's premier golf destinations — dramatic coastal and mountain scenery paired with a strong selection of quality courses, from Fancourt's three championship layouts to the clifftop drama of Pinnacle Point. It's ideal for multi-day tours.
  • Yes — we organise shuttles, inter-club timing, and accommodation logistics so your group can relax and focus on the golf. The Garden Route covers a lot of ground, and getting the logistics right makes a huge difference to the experience.
  • We recommend 3–5 days for a solid sample of the region's best courses. Seven days or more gives you the freedom to add safaris at Addo or Gondwana, longer coastal stops, and time to explore towns like Knysna, Plettenberg Bay, and George properly.
